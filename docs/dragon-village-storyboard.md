# Dragon Village — Story & Storyboard

> A companion narrative set within the Orzentia universe.
> Describes the origin, daily life, conflict, and inciting incident
> for the dragon-breeding village and its central family.

---

## World Context

The Kingdom of Orzentia is a rich and prosperous land. Its power and wealth
are built, in no small part, on the backs of a small clan who have perfected
the ancient art of dragon breeding over countless generations. The kingdom's
control over these dragons — their sheer strength, speed, flame, and
bondability — has allowed it to establish favorable trade routes with
neighboring nations, enforcing compliance through the implicit (and sometimes
explicit) threat of draconic reprisal.

---

## The Village

A small, isolated village nestled within the kingdom's borders is home to the
dragon-breeding clan. Life here moves at a different rhythm than the rest of
Orzentia — measured not in years, but in generations.

### The Breeding Cycle

- A single clutch of eggs takes **60 years to hatch** from laying to birth.
- The clan possesses a unique magical ability: they can imbue local natural
  resources — soil, water, mineral — with the precise nutrients required for
  draconic gestation.
- Eggs must be kept at extreme temperatures throughout the cycle.
- At the center of the village stands a ring of **massive stone furnace-obelisks**:
  ancient, ritualistic structures carved with clan iconography. Tenders keep
  them perpetually stoked, feeding them **magically-imbued jewels** that burn
  with an otherworldly heat and provide the precise arcane warmth each egg
  requires.
- After a clutch hatches, the burned jewel residue — *ashglass* — is salvaged
  by the junior members of the clan. Nothing is wasted.

### The Covenant

The village operates under a formal agreement with the kingdom, reaffirmed
in ceremony **every 25 years**:

| The Kingdom provides | The Village provides |
|---|---|
| Military protection from external threats | All dragons from a clutch, save one |
| Raw materials (jewels, ores) monthly during active breeding cycles | Expertise, labor, and magical stewardship |
| Provisions and trade access | Loyalty and discretion |

At each 25-year reaffirmation, **two children are exchanged** as
emissaries — one from a high-ranking family of the kingdom, one from the
clan — to spend formative years learning the other society's ways and serve
as a living symbol of the covenant. Over generations, these exchanges have
sometimes led to arranged marriages between emissary families, weaving the
two bloodlines loosely together.

## Central Characters

### The Father — Edren (working name)
Head Breeder of the clan. Quiet, methodical, deeply reverent toward the
dragons in his care. His entire identity is bound to the furnaces and the
long, patient work of the breeding cycles. His wife died years ago — the
village's healer, taken by an illness no dragon fire could cure. He has not
remarried. He speaks little of her, but her absence shapes everything.

### The Older Sister — Sela (working name)
The eldest child. She handles the clan's clerical work: records of egg
temperatures, breeding cycle logs, material requisition orders to the kingdom.
She is organized, pragmatic, and quietly furious at the terms of the
covenant — though she is too careful to say so openly.

### The Son (Player Character)
The younger sibling. His primary duty is **ashglass salvage** — picking
through the burned-out residue of exhausted jewel fuel after each stoke of
the furnaces. It is unglamorous work, the lowest rung of clan labor, but
it requires a sharp eye: improperly salvaged ashglass can contaminate future
fuel batches. He knows the furnaces better than anyone, knows their moods
and their heat.

### Squink
A **fish-cat chimera** — one of the king's experimental crossbreeds, since
discarded. Lean and low to the ground, with iridescent scales running his
spine and the slow amber eyes of a barn cat. He drifted into the village
some weeks ago and has not explained himself. The clan tolerates him
because he keeps to himself and causes no trouble.

He is aloof in the way that suggests he has seen things he does not intend
to discuss. He is not unfriendly — just unhurried. Occasionally he says
something that could mean two things, and does not wait to find out which
one you took it as.

He doesn't breathe fire. He doesn't fly. He is, by the kingdom's metrics,
a failure. He seems unbothered by this.

### Key Locations

| Location | Description |
|---|---|
| Furnace Ring | Center of the village. Seven ancient stone obelisks arranged in a ring. Each houses an egg chamber. The air shimmers here at all hours. |
| Salvage Yard | The ash-pit east of the ring where burned jewel residue collects. The son's workstation. Buckets, tools, ashglass fragments. |
| Archive Building | Stone structure with thick walls to protect the records. Sela's domain. Temperature logs, requisition orders, breeding lineage scrolls. |
| Furnace Tunnels | Deep-stone maintenance passages beneath the furnace ring. Cool, dark, and quiet. The son knows them by touch. |
| Village Gate | Simple wooden gate on the road south toward the capital. Where the kingdom messenger arrived. Where Kael returned. |

## Opening Game Sequence

The game begins **before dawn**. The player character is already at the
salvage yard, picking through the cooling ash from the night stoke.

1. **Tutorial prompt** — basic movement and interaction (F to talk) is
   introduced through the environment. A short text overlay establishes
   the setting before control is given to the player.

2. **Edren approaches** (or player walks to the furnace ring) — first
   mandatory conversation. Edren checks in, confirms the eggs are stable,
   and tells the player to finish the salvage. This is the player's
   introduction to the core loop of village life.

3. **Free exploration** — the player can speak to all five NPCs before
   the inciting event. Each NPC hints at the tension building around the
   kingdom messenger's visit.

4. **The messenger scene** — a scripted event where the messenger is
   seen leaving the council hall and riding out. Edren walks to the
   archive building to meet Sela. The player can follow or continue
   exploring.

5. **Kael returns** — if the player goes to the village gate, Kael is
   there, just arrived, looking shaken. He deflects questions but his
   arrival is clearly significant.

6. **Optional: find Squink** — Squink is tucked near the tunnel entrance.
   He will not seek out the player. If found, his three lines drop the
   first hint that the world beyond the village is not what the clan
   believes it to be.

## The Conflict

### Act One — Pressure from the Throne

The current king, ruling from the capital, is bleeding his citizens dry.
Heavy taxation, lavish court spending, and a growing network of nobles
enriched at the expense of common people have pushed the kingdom to a
breaking point. Villages across the region are organizing. Some have
formed quiet alliances with neighboring nations that have long chafed
under Orzentia's trade coercion.

Word reaches the capital: a coalition is forming.

The king's response is immediate — he needs more dragons. More, faster.

He sends a formal demand to the village: **accelerate the next clutch**.

Edren presents the demand to the clan council. The answer is delivered
back to the capital politely but without ambiguity: *the cycle cannot be
rushed. The eggs require sixty years. This is not a policy — it is biology.*

The king accepts the answer. Officially.

### Act Two — The Experiments

Quietly, the king commissions his own program. Away from the village, away
from the covenant, his scholars and soldiers begin attempting to breed
dragons with other species — wolves, large serpents, birds of prey, deep-sea
fish. The goal is volume: creatures that are *good enough*, available
*now*, in numbers that could offset the slow sacred cycle.

The results are grotesque and painful. Most subjects do not survive.
Some produce offspring — **chimeras** — creatures of mixed heritage and
wildly inconsistent traits. None possess the full suite of qualities that
make the sacred dragons militarily decisive: the sacred dragons' flame
intensity, their ride-weight tolerance, their near-supernatural **bondability**
with riders. But chimeras are alive, and they are growing, and they can be
conscripted.

Squink is one of these chimeras. He escapes — or is discarded as a
failed specimen — and wanders until he finds the village. He does not
explain where he came from. He sits near the furnace tunnels. He stays.

### Act Three — The Village Learns

One of the 25-year emissary exchanges brings the truth home. A young clan
member, recently returned from their years in the capital, has seen the
chimera pens. They carry documentation — papers Sela recognizes, formatted
using the same kingdom requisition codes she processes every month, but
filed against accounts the clan was never told existed.

The clan holds council. Edren is asked to deliver an ultimatum.

The message to the king is direct: **end the experiments, or the covenant
is dissolved**. No more dragons. No more cooperation. No more silence.

### Act Four — The Burning

The king's reply is not a letter.

It is soldiers, and it is fire.

The village is attacked before dawn. The furnace-obelisks — centuries old,
irreplaceable — are toppled. The ashglass stores are scattered. The
records Sela kept, every log of every cycle going back to her great-great-
grandmother, burn with the archive building.

Most of the village does not survive.

Edren survives because he is in the deep-stone maintenance tunnels beneath
the furnaces when the soldiers arrive. The son survives because Edren
pulls him in and seals the tunnel access.

They emerge into smoke and ruin.

### Act Five — The Aftermath

In the days after the attack, Edren and the son move through the wreckage
salvaging what little can be saved — ashglass shards, a few unbroken egg
temperature logs, Sela's personal cipher key found under a collapsed beam.

The furnace-obelisks are rubble. The work of generations is ash.

Squink finds them in the ruins. He does not say where he hid during the
attack. He sits near the son in the silence, and does not leave.

They have each other, Squink, and what they can carry. The question of
what comes next hangs between them, unanswered.

## Open Questions / To Develop

- What is the son's name?
- Does Sela survive? (Her death in the attack could be a significant motivator,
  or her survival — scattered into hiding — could be a reunion plot point.)
- What does the one surviving dragon egg mean for the story? Does the village
  retain it? Is it hidden in the tunnels?
- What is the exact nature of Squink's bond with the son — is it a diminished
  version of the sacred dragon bondability, or something entirely new? Does
  Squink himself know?
- How does this village narrative connect to the broader Orzentia main story
  (the Ameni, the Binding, King Andrien)?
- What role do the neighboring nations — the ones building the coalition against
  the king — play in the son's eventual path?
- Arranged marriages between emissary families: is there an emissary the son
  knows who is now in the capital? Someone whose position is now complicated
  by the massacre?
